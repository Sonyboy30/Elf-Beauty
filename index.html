Q4 FY2026 (Reported May 20, 2026)
e.l.f. Beauty Beats Q4; Rhode Delivers $390M in Net Sales; 29th Consecutive Quarter of Growth
  • Q4 net sales grew 35% YoY to $449.3M, beating the $423M consensus estimate. Rhode contributed ~$113M (34 ppts of growth); organic growth ex-Rhode was ~+1% YoY, within the flat-to-2% guidance range.
  • Full-year FY2026 net sales reached $1.636B (+24.6%), adjusted EBITDA $335M (+13%), and adjusted EPS $3.13. Tariffs averaged 55% in FY2026, more than double the prior year — the #1 headwind.
  • Rhode surpassed $500M in global retail sales, achieved 80%+ net sales growth, and became the #1 beauty brand at Sephora North America. Non-e.l.f. brands now represent 30% of global consumption.
  • FY2027 guidance: net sales growth of 12–14% ($1.84–1.87B), adj EBITDA $379–385M, adj EPS $3.27–$3.32. Guidance assumes ~35% tariff rate (vs. 55% in FY2026).
"Fiscal 26 marked our 7th consecutive year of net sales and market share gains, and our 29th consecutive quarter of growth. We've built a powerful, disruptive brand portfolio with significant whitespace ahead." — Tarang Amin, Chairman & CEO, e.l.f. Beauty

BUY Consensus (17 analysts)
e.l.f. Beauty is a rare consumer franchise that has compounded 25%+ annually for seven years. The stock's collapse from $151 to $53 (-65%) reflects tariff fear, GAAP earnings distortion from acquisition accounting, and general risk-off in high-multiple consumer names — not fundamental deterioration. The business generated $1.64B in revenue and $215M in FCF in FY2026, Rhode is firing on all cylinders, and FY2027 guidance implies 12–14% organic growth with adj EPS of $3.27–3.32 (forward P/E of ~14x — near a decade low). The primary risk is tariff escalation beyond the 35% assumed rate. If tariffs remain manageable, ELF looks significantly undervalued at current levels. Moderate risk score of 52 reflects the balancing act between structural growth and near-term macro headwinds.
